# Motivation

## Who I am
I’m a Computer Science student at the University of Warwick with a strong interest in building systems that model complex real-world processes. My experience is primarily in Python, machine learning, and simulation-based projects.

Recently, I developed an airport traffic simulation that models queueing, prioritisation, and resource allocation between aircraft and runways. This project introduced me to challenges around coordinating many interacting entities, which naturally led me to explore agent-based modeling.

---

## Why Mesa
I was drawn to Mesa because it provides a structured and extensible way to build agent-based models in Python. Compared to custom simulation code, Mesa offers abstractions that make it easier to reason about agent interactions, scheduling, and system dynamics.

I discovered Mesa while looking for better ways to model multi-agent systems and have started working through the tutorials and building initial models to understand how the framework operates in practice.

---

## What I want to learn
I’m particularly interested in how Mesa handles:
- scheduling and coordination between agents  
- shared resources and constraints  
- designing scalable and maintainable simulation architectures  

More broadly, I want to deepen my understanding of agent-based modeling as a tool for representing complex systems and emergent behaviour, and learn how to design reusable components within such systems.

---

## Where I want to go
My goal is to contribute to Mesa by improving how complex interactions and resource-constrained systems can be modeled. In particular, I’m interested in contributing features or examples related to scheduling, resource allocation, and realistic system simulations.

I also aim to contribute example models that demonstrate practical use cases of Mesa beyond traditional academic settings, bridging the gap between research-oriented models and engineering-style simulations.
